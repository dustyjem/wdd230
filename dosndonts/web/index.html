Our mission was to survey students attending Brigham Young University – Idaho and put together useful information in a way that would help give incoming and current students ideas on how to make their time at this university easier.

Here are some Dos & Don'ts

Education

  • 1

    The majority of students in the survey mentioned that they prefer to take somewhere between 12-15 credits a semester during their time at BYU-I.

  • 2

    70 % of students surveyed, stated that they would have preferred to spread their general education classes across multiple semesters.

  • 3

    Students recommended joining the clubs that are offered through their majors, as they provide great resources and can help connect you to internships and jobs after school

  • 4

    Students stated that connecting with classmates was a great way to obtain success at BYU-I, and it helps create future connections in the business world after school

  • 5

    Students are recommended to click Here when registering for classes. This website rates professors from BYU-I on multiple criteria and has been very helpful for students in finding the most liked professors.

  • 6

    Avoid taking just 1 class a day as it can be hard to motivate yourself to go onto campus for just 1 class

  • 7

    Don’t put off looking for internships during your time at BYU-I, the university has a lot of resources that can help get you ready and into an internship

    Social

  • 1

    Don’t ignore the activities that BYU-I provides on campus. Students stated that they wished they would have participated more in the activities that BYU-I provided for its students. A great way to find out about the activities going on at BYU-I is by downloading the BYU-I I Belong app, which has the activities available on a daily schedule

  • 2

    Each semester at BYU-I provides various outdoor activities that you can do in Rexburg and the surrounding area. Fall: Haunted houses/mazes, Early fall season hikes, Winter: sledding at the dunes, snowboarding/skiing, snowshoeing. Spring: Hiking, river floating, mountain biking, outdoor sports, cave exploring

  • 3

    Don’t wait for friends to find you, find them. Students said that they had an easier time making friends at BYU-I when they made an effort to participate in anything that came their way.

  • 4

    When asked about fun ideas for FHE activities at BYU-I, students mentioned the following: game nights, going to the dunes, nerf wars, ax throwing, floating the river, cooking competitions, bowling, karaoke, ice skating painting, team-building exercises, potlucks, cave exploring, themed dinner parties.

  • 5

    Students also wished they would have had a better balance in their social, academic, and emotional life. BYU-I has great resources that can help in doing just that. Just click Here

Spiritual

  1. Majority of the students surveyed felt that attending devotional was a great opportunity for them to relieve some stress as well as be enrichened by the spirit that was felt during the devotionals

  2. Students said that when they chose whether to take religion classes online or in-person that they would prefer to take them in-person. Some online students stated that they just felt like they were going through the motions at times and weren’t getting much out of the classes as they did when they took them in person

  3. It was recommended to develop a good relationship with the leaders from your ward. Having a good relationship with your leaders makes everything so much easier because you feel like you have a confidant that you can talk about anything with, be it financial, spiritual, or so on

  4. Some students surveyed said that when they participated in callings and assignments, they felt a feeling of belonging and had a higher confidence level because of it. They were also able to make more friends and help bless others because of it. One student recommended asking the bishop for an assignment if they hadn’t given you one at the beginning of the semester.

  5. Don’t drop the ball on your ministering! Some students said that they had been very blessed by their ministers during hard times, and had wished that people were better at doing their ministering
